## Key Takeaways

1. **3-shot routine is essential**: Whole plant, affected area close-up, and leaf detail - this combination eliminates 80% of misdiagnoses
2. **Natural light beats flash**: Shoot near a window in indirect light for most accurate color representation
3. **Context matters**: Show the whole plant so AI understands normal vs. abnormal growth patterns
4. **Triage before diagnosis**: 60-second visual assessment tells you what photos to take and what to ask the AI
5. **Cross-reference results**: Compare AI recommendations with established guides before acting
6. **Privacy is optional**: You can get accurate diagnoses without uploading to cloud services
7. **Escalate when uncertain**: Some problems need human expertise - know when to call a pro

## Your AI Diagnosis Toolkit

### Essential Equipment

- **Smartphone with good camera**: 12MP+ recommended, clean lens
- **Natural light source**: Window with indirect light or shade outdoors
- **Neutral background**: White paper or light-colored surface for close-ups
- **Magnification**: Clip-on macro lens for tiny pests (optional but helpful)

### Photo Checklist

For every diagnosis session, capture:

1. **Whole plant shot**: Shows overall condition, environment, neighboring plants
2. **Problem area close-up**: Fills frame with affected leaves/stems, clear focus
3. **Detail shot**: Macro of specific symptom - spots, webbing, pests, etc.

**Bonus shots when relevant:**

- Underside of leaves (for pests)
- Soil surface and drainage holes
- Recent growth compared to old growth
- Root system if recently disturbed

### When to Skip AI and Call an Expert

**Immediate expert consultation needed:**

- Rapid plant collapse (hours, not days)
- Black, mushy stem base spreading upward
- Foul smell from soil or stems
- Suspected rare pest or disease
- Valuable or rare plant showing symptoms
- Symptoms affecting entire collection
- AI gives contradictory results

**AI works well for:**

- Common houseplant problems
- Gradual symptom development
- Clear visual symptoms
- Identification of common pests
- General care guidance
- Learning about plant needs
